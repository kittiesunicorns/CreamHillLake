Membership FAQ

Questions about joining the Cream Hill Lake Association

Anyone seeking membership needs to be nominated by a current member. The nominator must acquire three "seconding" letters supporting the nomination. These are sent to the Membership Committee Chair for approval by the full membership committee.
A prospective member must be at least 18 years old. Two individuals who "generally reside together" (married couples, couples living together, with or without children) may be sponsored under one membership. The nominee must meet one of the following residency requirements: be a full-time resident of Cornwall, own a dwelling in Cornwall for personal use, or physically occupy a dwelling in Cornwall for at least 60 days during the calendar year.
Any member in good standing may nominate a person for membership. Emeritus Members and members of the Membership Committee may not nominate or second nominations.
There is no limit to the number of nominations a member can make. However, each person in a membership may nominate or second only one nominee per year, and may not both write letters for the same nominee.
The nominator must email the Membership Chair to officially nominate someone. The email should include the nominating letter, three seconding letters (from three different memberships), and the completed nominating form. Send completed applications to Membership Committee Chair Susie Ott: lillyott@yahoo.com.
Per Section 4.1 of the bylaws, letters must not be form letters and must state the full name(s), address(es), and phone number(s) of the nominee; how long the writer has known the nominee; a description of the nominee's family; how the nominee meets the membership requirements; and why, in the writer's opinion, the nominee is personally responsible and compatible with the membership. Please also include the nominee's email address.
A member in good standing who has been a member for ten or more years may nominate their child for membership. The standard nominating letter applies, but no seconding letters are required.
An application is complete when the nominating letter, three seconding letters, and the Nomination Form have all been received by the Membership Chair. It is the responsibility of the nominator — not the Membership Committee or the nominee — to ensure the application is complete.
The Membership Committee Chair files completed applications in the order received. The committee meets yearly, usually in May, to act on applications. Upon approval, new memberships are placed on a waiting list in order of receipt. Those approved will be invited to join when a space becomes available, provided they still meet the membership requirements.
  • Write letters that help the committee get to know the nominees — not form letters.
  • Send all letters together in one email to avoid incomplete applications.
  • Submit the nominator's form to make sure nothing is missing.
  • You are under no obligation to nominate or second someone just because you were asked. If you don't know the applicant well enough, it's fine to decline.

Ready to nominate someone? Use the nomination form or contact Susie Ott.
